Caring for Your Alaskan Dog

Training

The training for these breeds will depend entirely on what they will be doing on a day to day basis. If they will be used as a sled dog, they will need extremely specialized training in order to work well with their team and understand basic sledding commands. If they will be for pleasure and companionship, their training should be like all other breeds: basic commands, leash training, socialization training, and food aggression training. Above all, these breeds have a pack mentality and it is important that they recognize you as the alpha of the pack otherwise they may be a handful.

Grooming

All of our dogs will require daily brushing at the very least. They should also be regularly washed and groomed to avoid health concerns. The following benefits to Grooming are seen below:

  1. It emoves dirt and debris build-up
  2. It can keep the house cleaner during shedding seasons
  3. It provides a first line of defense against tangles and mats in the fur
  4. It will allow for early flea/tick/infection detection
  5. It spreads natural oils to help moisturize the skin and keep the coat shiny

Health

Alaskan Husky

  1. -Average Life Span: 10-13 Years
  2. -Common Health Issues: lysosomal storage disease, various eye problems, potential hip dysplasia
  3. -Average Weight: 38-50 Pounds

Alaskan Malamute

  1. -Average Life Span: 10-12 Years
  2. -Common Health Issues: hip dysplasia, cataracts(hereditary), potential seizure disorders
  3. -Average Weight: 71-95 Pounds

Alaskan Klee Kai

  1. -Average Life Span: 12-16 Years
  2. -Common Health Issues: hip dysplasia, potential digestion issues
  3. -Average Weight: 16-22 Pounds

American Eskimo

  1. -Average Life Span: 13-15 Years
  2. -Common Health Issues: potential allergies that can cause skin sensitivity and pyruvate kinase deficiency, which can potentially cause chronic anemia
  3. -Average Weight: 18-35 Pounds

Siberian Husky

  1. -Average Life Span: 12-15 Years
  2. -Common Health Issues: mostly genetic health issues exist such as possible seizures and eye defects
  3. -Average Weight: 35-60 Pounds

Samoyed

  1. -Average Life Span: 12-14 Years
  2. -Common Health Issues: a genetic kidney disease called glomerulopathy is possible in this breed
  3. -Average Weight: 35-66 Pounds

Greenland

  1. -Average Life Span: 10-13 Years
  2. -Common Health Issues: though generally healthy, this breed can be prone to hip dysplasia and gastric torsion
  3. -Average Weight: 66-71 Pounds

Seppala Siberian Sled Dog

  1. -Average Life Span: 12-15 Years
  2. -Common Health Issues: this breed can be prone to allergies, cancer and issues with their eyes
  3. -Average Weight: 40-53 Pounds

Nutrition

Although a healthy diet can take a variety of forms, we suggest either a high-quality kibble or a raw diet consisting of either fish, fowl, or grassland annimals as the protein, probiotics to aid in digestion, and other vitamins and minerals derived from natural sources.
