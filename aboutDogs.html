About Alaskan Dogs

Alaskan Husky

This wonderful breed is a blend of several Nordic breeds. These dogs are typically used as sled dogs. Their ability to pull heavy weight and work as a team is the primary goal of any reputable breeder such as ourselves.

Alaskan Malamute

This large breed is famous not only for their strengh and endurance, but also for their friendly and playful behavior. Though they are similar to other Alaskan breeds, the malamute may be more suited for a citizen lifestyle rather than other working breeds.

Alaskan Klee Kai

This smaller breed was originally designed as a smaller, more domesticated version of both the Alaskan and Siberian Husky. They are very intelligent, energetic, and will need constant stimulation so it does not get bored.

American Eskimo

This is another smaller dog meant for companionship rather than heavy duty work. The origin of the breed can be traced back to Germany as it has the German Spitz in the bloodline. These dogs are quite intelligent and friendly by design.

Siberian Husky

This breed is medium-sized and is ideally used for endurance trips pulling light cargo on a team of sled dogs. Although friendly, this breed is also loyal and powerful.

Samoyed

This breed is primarially used for herding. Their origins come from Siberia where they helped their owners heard reindeer. Though they are playful, they can also be quite stubborn so training is important.

Greenland

This breed is similar to a husky, and is also used as a sled dog. They originated in Siberia but were largely popularized in Greenland and Denmark. They are independent animals but also work well on a team.

Seppala Siberian Sled Dog

This meduim-sized breed is rather rare in our world today. Their primary breeding purpose was to be a sled dog and work as a team pulling weight in the cold country of Canada.
